# ScreeMy

Screen recording for macOS with a floating camera bubble, laser pointer, and click effects — built for educators, streamers, and anyone who wants to look more professional on screen.

---

## ⚠️ Security Warning (Gatekeeper)

ScreeMy is an independent (indie) app and is not distributed through the Mac App Store. Because of this, macOS may show a warning saying the app is from an unidentified developer — **this is normal and expected**.

**To open the app:**

1. Right-click (or Control-click) `ScreeMy.app` and choose **Open**
2. Click **Open** in the dialog that appears

Or go to **System Settings → Privacy & Security** and click **Open Anyway** after attempting to launch.

---

## Required Permissions

ScreeMy will ask for the following permissions on first use:

| Permission | Why |
|---|---|
| **Screen Recording** | To capture your display or a specific window |
| **Camera** | To show your face in the floating bubble overlay |
| **Microphone** | To record audio alongside the video |

You can grant these in **System Settings → Privacy & Security**.

---

## Features

### Recording
- Record your **entire display** or a **specific app window**
- Output saved as an MP4 file to a location you choose

### Camera Bubble
- Floating camera overlay that stays on top while recording
- **Shapes:** Circle, Square, Portrait
- **Sizes:** Normal, Small
- **Border color:** fully customizable
- Drag the bubble anywhere on screen during recording

### Laser Pointer
- Hold **⌥ Option** and move your mouse to draw a red laser trail on screen
- The trail fades automatically after 2 seconds
- Great for pointing out things during demos or tutorials

### Click Effects
- Visual pulse rings appear on screen whenever you click
- **Left click** → yellow ring
- **Right click** → blue ring
- Can be toggled on or off in the dashboard

### Menu Bar Icon
- ScreeMy lives in your menu bar while running
- During recording: click the red icon to stop and save
- Not recording: click to open the dashboard or quit
